05 — Scientific Rigor

Where myth meets mechanism

Bayesian reasoning, cognitive science, environmental determinism, fractal pattern detection, and scholarly debate visualization.

Why do unrelated cultures invent the same stories? There are exactly two explanations: either they learned from each other (transmission), or human brains are wired to produce the same ideas independently (cognitive universals). Both are testable hypotheses. We built tools for each — plus instruments that probe the physical environment (geography, astronomy, climate), the mathematical structure (fractals, self-similarity), and the documentary provenance (source criticism with epistemic weighting from 23 scholars) of mythological systems. Bayesian confidence propagation ensures that uncertainty compounds correctly through chains of inference.

Bayesian Confidence Propagation

Uncertainty that compounds honestly

When Claim A (75% confident) supports Claim B (80% confident), the combined confidence isn't 80% — it's lower, because the supporting evidence is itself uncertain. Our Bayesian propagation engine handles this correctly through every chain of inference, so you never mistake stacked weak evidence for one strong proof.

10 — Geographic Intelligence

Ideas don't teleport. They travel.

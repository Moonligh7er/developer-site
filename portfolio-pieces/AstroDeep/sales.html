Questions

You're probably thinking...

"Isn't astrology just confirmation bias?"
That's exactly what AstroDeep tests. The blind test protocol removes all labels — you don't know which statement came from your chart. If you consistently pick the real one above chance, that's interesting data. If you can't, that's also interesting data. Either way, you'll know.
"Do I need to know astrology to use this?"
Not at all. AstroDeep explains everything as you go. There's a built-in glossary with 40+ terms across astrology, statistics, biology, and psychology. The app is designed for curious people, not astrologers.
"What if the app proves astrology doesn't work?"
Then you'll know — with real data, not someone else's opinion. And you'll still have 16 biological layers of scientifically-grounded birth-timing effects that do have peer-reviewed evidence. The bio-layers don't need astrology to be true.
"How is this different from Co-Star or The Pattern?"
Those apps tell you what your chart means. AstroDeep tests whether that meaning holds up — with Granger causality testing, prediction calibration curves, adversarial steel-man arguments, and a falsification engine that systematically hunts for contradictions. They're horoscope generators. This is a personal research station with 55+ statistical engines. A fundamentally different category of product.
"Is my data safe?"
Your data lives on your device by default (Signal tier). Beacon, Pulsar, and Pulsar+ add optional cloud sync via Supabase with row-level security. Anonymized, aggregated data may contribute to population statistics — you control this in settings.
"Why should I pay when the science is free?"

Beacon ($6/mo) gives you Sonnet AI + Google Cloud voice. Pulsar ($13/mo) upgrades to Opus AI. Pulsar+ ($19/mo) adds ElevenLabs natural voice. The scientific core never goes behind a paywall.
"I don't know my exact birth time."
That's fine. AstroDeep works without birth time — you just won't get Rising sign, house placements, or the most time-sensitive layers. The Counterfactual Explorer even shows you how sensitive your chart is to birth time, so you can see what you're missing.

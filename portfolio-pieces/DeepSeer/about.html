About Deep Seer

Making public data accessible, accountable, and actionable.

Our mission:

Deep Seer exists to democratize access to open-source intelligence. We believe that the data governments and institutions publish about themselves — environmental records, financial disclosures, regulatory actions, satellite feeds — should be accessible to everyone, not just those with enterprise budgets and technical teams.

What We Do

Deep Seer is a real-time geospatial intelligence platform that aggregates 90+ live public data feeds onto a single interactive 3D globe. We combine satellite tracking, flight monitoring, maritime vessel data, environmental sensors, political finance records, and dozens of other public data sources with AI-powered analysis tools.

Our users include investigative journalists, environmental researchers, human rights organizations, academic researchers, civic technologists, corporate compliance teams, and government analysts. They use Deep Seer to:

  • Monitor environmental pollution and hold polluters accountable
  • Track the flow of political money from donors to policy outcomes
  • Investigate corporate ownership structures and offshore entities
  • Document human rights violations with evidence-grade chain of custody
  • Analyze military and security patterns using open-source data
  • Research the intersection of poverty, pollution, and health outcomes

Our Values

Transparency

Every data source is cited. Every analysis method is documented. Every AI output includes confidence scores. We show our work.

Accountability

We built 49 cross-domain correlation rules specifically to surface conflicts of interest, regulatory capture, and environmental injustice.

Privacy by Design

Face detection runs entirely in-browser — we never see the data. We use auth-only cookies. We don't track users with advertising pixels.

Accessibility

WCAG 2.1 AA compliant. Free tier includes all 90+ data sources. We believe intelligence tools shouldn't be gatekept by price.

Responsible Use

Deep Seer provides powerful analytical capabilities including military aircraft tracking, CCTV camera aggregation, vessel monitoring, and face detection. We take the responsible use of these tools seriously:

  • All data sources are public. We aggregate data that governments and organizations have chosen to make publicly available — ADS-B transponder data, EPA discharge records, FEC filings, DOT traffic cameras, etc.
  • Face detection requires explicit consent and runs entirely in the user's browser. No biometric data is ever transmitted to our servers.
  • Our Terms of Service explicitly prohibit stalking, harassment, mass surveillance, discriminatory profiling, and any use that violates civil rights laws.
  • Evidence integrity features (SHA-256 hashing, RFC 3161 timestamping) are designed for accountability and legal proceedings, not for circumventing them.
  • CCTV feeds are public DOT traffic cameras operated by government transportation departments for public safety. We do not access private security cameras.

We designed Deep Seer as a tool for accountability — for journalists investigating corruption, for researchers studying environmental injustice, for NGOs documenting violations. Our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy reflect this mission.

Data Sources

Deep Seer aggregates data from 90+ public sources including:

  • US Government agencies: USGS, NOAA, NASA, EPA, FAA, FEC, SEC, FBI, CDC, DOL, NRC, OSHA
  • International organizations: GDACS, ACLED, SIPRI, ICIJ, Fund for Peace, World Bank
  • Open data networks: OpenSky Network, AISStream, CelesTrak, OpenStreetMap, Global Forest Watch
  • Academic institutions: Georgia Tech (IODA), Princeton (Eviction Lab), UNC (News Deserts)

For a complete list, see our Features page. All data source licenses and attribution are documented in our Privacy Policy.

Technology

Deep Seer is built with:

  • Frontend: React, TypeScript, CesiumJS (3D globe), Zustand (state management)
  • Backend: Node.js, Express, PostgreSQL, Prisma ORM
  • AI: Anthropic Claude API (Haiku for fast tasks, Sonnet for reasoning)
  • Infrastructure: Render (hosting), Stripe (billing), Sentry (monitoring)
